Lotus pose










Instructions


  1. Start by sitting on the floor with legs out straight.
  2. Firmly root down through your sitz bones.
  3. Bend the right knee and bring the foot across the body towards the left elbow.
  4. If you can, hook your right foot in the left elbow and the right knee in the right elbow, bringing the hands together and creating a cradle for the leg and foot.
  5. You can gently rock the leg back and forth to open the hip up a little more.
  6. Then, bring the right foot down so the outside edge rests in the left groin.
  7. Leaning back slightly, lift the right leg off the ground and bend the left knee.
  8. Swiveling at the left hip, bring the left foot across the body and into the right groin.
  9. Firmly press your heels into your belly.
  10. Sit tall, keeping your spine long.
  11. You can rest your palms on your knees, facing up or down.
  12. If this pose is comfortable, stay here for a meditation or a couple minutes of deep breathing.
  13. Make sure to practice on both sides.

Benefits


Lotus Pose Benefits: When mastered, Lotus Pose (Padmasana) allows the practitioner to hold his body completely steady for long periods of time. As the body and mind are connected and control each other, steadiness of the body brings steadiness of the mind. This steadiness is the first step towards productive meditation.
